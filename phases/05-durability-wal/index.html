Phase 5

Durability: journal, WAL, recovery

Everything so far assumed writes land cleanly. They don't. A power cut mid-write can leave a page half-updated, a freelist pointing at a live page, a tree split applied on one side only. This phase closes the gap between "I called write" and "the data is safe and consistent after a crash." You'll study the two classic mechanisms — the rollback journal and the write-ahead log — then build a checksummed WAL, its checkpoint path, and a recovery routine that runs on every open.

Why this comes after records & free space

You now have a multi-page engine where one logical change (an insert that splits a node, allocates an overflow page, and pops the freelist) touches several pages. That is exactly the situation durability must protect: a change is only safe if all of its pages survive a crash together, or none do. You couldn't feel that need until you had multi-page operations.

What you'll be able to do after Phase 5

Reason precisely about what a crash can and cannot do to a file; implement a write-ahead log that makes multi-page transactions atomic and durable; checkpoint the log back into the main file; and run a recovery pass on open that restores the last committed state for crashes covered by the documented storage contract.
