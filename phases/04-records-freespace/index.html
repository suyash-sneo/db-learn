Phase 4

Records & free space

Phase 3 stored small, inline entries by bounding their size. Real data is messy: variable-length records, documents bigger than a page, deletions that leave holes. This phase makes your storage honest — slotted pages for variable-length records, overflow pages for big ones, compaction for the holes, and a freelist so freed pages get reused instead of growing the file forever.

Why this comes after the B+tree

You needed a working tree first to feel the pain: bounded entry sizes, no space reclamation, pages that fragment as you delete. Phase 4 lifts those restrictions. Slotted pages and the freelist are not glamorous, but they are what separates a toy from a database that can run for months without bloating.

What you'll be able to do after Phase 4

Store variable-length records in slotted pages, spill oversized values to overflow-page chains, reclaim space within a page by compaction, and maintain a persistent freelist of reusable pages — then wire it all into a small record store.
